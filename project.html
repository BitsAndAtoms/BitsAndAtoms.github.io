Digital Holography

Digital holography for rapid analysis of particle fields in microchannel flows. Holography provides access to volumetric information from a single image. We have implemented mili-second scale reconstruction using high performance parallel computing.

Holo-rheology

Holography can quantitate shear and extensional fields in a microchannel. It can be used for exotic flow visualization such as purely elastic instabilities in a serpentine channel that display spatially smooth and random in time flows. Shown above are hyperbolic, serpentine and linear flows

Automated microfluidic viscometer

Tapered channel to calculate viscosity of complex fluids using a single flow rate and a single picture of the device.The interface location is used to calulate viscosity of unknown fluid with changing shear rate in the channel. This method uses ~ 200 microlitres of fluid and costs less than $5.

Traumatic brain injury device

Approximately 40% of NFL players suffer brain injury related diseases such as Alzhimer's in later life. We designed a microfluidic device to induce brain injury in the model nematode C.elegans. The video shows the worm colliding with 25G force in a microfluidic channel.

Fluid flow simulation

Entry level flows were simulated for a 60 degree constriction using finite element method in Ansys Fluent. The above simulation is for a power law fluid flowing at steady state in a microchannel. We aim to conduct digital holography of such unexplored flows in the case of elastic fluids. Entry level flow behavior is a ubiquitous but highly unresearched flow phenomena.

Digital inline holography microscopy

The digital holography setup I maintained during my PhD is capable of high speed imaging of complex flows and biological matter such as cancer cells and micro-organisms. I have assembeled this setup many times over and have complete understanding of its operation including laser usage and microscopy. We partially succeeded in combining high speed processing to enable real time analysis.
