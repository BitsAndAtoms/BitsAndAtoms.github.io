A bit about me

I am a researcher at TTU engaged in digital holography and microfluidics to investigate physics of complex systems.

I harness the power of JAVA and MATLAB to implement image processing, machine learning, tracking and simulations.

My current interests lie in exploring fluid mechanics, large data sets, advanced scientific programming and parralel computing.

I am looking for opportunities in applied research programmes, startups and full-time jobs.

Career objective

I want to apply computation and first principles based physics approaches to engineer new technologies.

My journey

I hail from the heart of India but consider myself to be equal parts Texan and Indian. My interest in technology gravitated me to America where I started research in complex fluids and active matter. During this time I gained experience in microfabrication and fluid mechanics of complex fluids.

My research is focussed on digital holography for mapping 3D motion specifically. This can help, for example, in better understanding of fluid flow to improve manufacturing process efficiency or to better understand 3D muscle driven locomotion which can be used for genetic screening.

I have devloped an immense interest in programming applicaitons and therefore I am studying computer science in parallel by taking courses in programming, parralel computing and image processing.
